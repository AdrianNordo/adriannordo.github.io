Game of Thrones

The Game of Thrones television series is an adaptation of George R. R. Martin's A Song of Ice and Fire , but is different in some ways. The series is produced by HBO based on the Song of Ice and Fire novels written by George R.R. Martin, debuting in April 2011. Created by David Benioff and D. B. Weiss for HBO. The show was both produced and filmed in Belfast and elsewhere in the United Kingdom. Filming locations also included Canada, Croatia, Iceland, Malta, Morocco, and Spain. The series premiered on April 17, 2011, and concluded on May 19, 2019, with a total of 73 over eight seasons.

The series is set on the fictional continents of Westeros & Essos, Game of Thrones has a large cast and follows several story arcs. One is about the Throne of Westeros and follows a web of alliances and conflicts among the noble dynasties, either wanting to claim the throne for themselves or fighting for independence from it. Another focuses of the last descendant of the realm's deposed ruling dynasty (House Targaryen) who has been exiled to Essos and is plotting a return to the throne. The last story arc follows A bastard and his journey in the Night's Watch, a brotherhood defending the realm against the white walkers, but not people in Westeros believe they exist.

Game of Thrones attracted a record-breaking viewership on Home Box Office (HBO) and has a broad, active, and international fan base. The series has been praised for its acting, complex characters, story, scope, and production values, although its frequent use of nudity and violence was criticized. The final season, especially its four last episodes, received significant criticism for its condenses story and creative decisions, the last episodes of the series very low scores, the last episode has a 4/10 score on IMDb, with many considering it a disappointing conclusion.
