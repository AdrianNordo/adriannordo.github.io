House Targaryen

House Targaryen is the name of the oldest family in Westeros. Westeros is a content in the fantasy world of A Song of Ice and Fire by George R. R. Martin. In Westeros it is accustomed to having a family name and sigil, the family name and sigil is something to be proud of. The Targaryen sigil is a three-headed dragon breathing flames, red on black. The house words are "Fire and Blood". It eventually became the first royal house of the Seven Kingdoms, as House Targaryen of King's Landing.'

House Targaryen is the only family of dragonlords who survived the Doom of Valyria, the Targaryens left Valyria about twelve years before the Doom. They resided for multiple generations on the island of Dragonstone, until Aegon The Conqueror and his sisters (who were also his wife's), Visenya and Rhaenys, began the first of the Wars of Conquest in 2 BC "Before the Conquest"

During Aegon's Conquest, the Targaryens build the Aegonfort in the new capital King's Landing, but the Aegonfort didn't last long and was later torn down and the Red Keep Was built.
The Red Keep remained their main seat for the duration of their dynasty, and which house the Iron Throne. Their original caste "Dragonstone" was almost always given to the heir to the Throne, the Prince of Dragonstone.

House Targaryen ruled the Seven Kingdoms of Westeros as the Kings of the Andals, the Rhoynar, and the First Men, the Lords of the Seven Kingdoms, and the Great House of the crownlands for nearly three hundred years. Seventeen male Targaryens Ruled from the Iron Throne. The dynasty ended with the death of Aerys Targaryen "The Mad King" during Robert's Rebellion, in which the Targaryens were overthrown and replaced by House Baratheon. The two surviving children of Aerys, Viserys and Daenerys Targaryen, fled into exile, and have resided in the Free Cities of Essos ever since.
